Honest read: Variant B (2-col pills) confirms your gut — 6 numbers don't fit a 187px card without feeling cramped. Variant C (1-col) is genuinely comfortable but scrolls more than today, especially on putts weeks (each player needs a second pill row).

So P2 is a real tradeoff, not a clear win — which is exactly why it's the one I said to prototype, not assume. Two lighter alternatives that keep today's compact box but kill the keyboard's downsides:
• Keep the 2-col number box, fix its two real bugs only: allow 2 digits (so a 10/11 is typeable) and update the cache on save so re-focus doesn't wipe inputs. No layout change at all.
• Hybrid: number box stays, but a tiny inline –/+ stepper under each for one-handed nudge near par, keyboard optional.

My recommendation: don't do full pills. Do the compact-box bug-fixes (2-digit + cache) which you'd never even notice as a redesign — they just remove the papercuts. Open this file on your phone to feel the real widths before deciding.
